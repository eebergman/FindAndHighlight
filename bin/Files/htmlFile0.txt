     <p><b>WrestleMania 32</b> was the thirty-second annual <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania" title="WrestleMania">WrestleMania</a> <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestling</a> <a href="/wiki/Pay-per-view" title="Pay-per-view">pay-per-view</a> (PPV) event produced by <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">WWE</a>. It took place on April 3, 2016, at <a href="/wiki/AT%26T_Stadium" title="AT&amp;T Stadium">AT&amp;T Stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Arlington,_Texas" title="Arlington, Texas">Arlington</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Twelve matches were contested (with three matches occurring on the WrestleMania Kickoff pre-show - two airing live on the <a href="/wiki/USA_Network" title="USA Network">USA Network</a>, which televised the second hour of the pre-show).</p> 
     <p>Four matches were considered marquee attractions:<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In the main event, <a href="/wiki/Roman_Reigns" title="Roman Reigns">Roman Reigns</a> defeated <a href="/wiki/Triple_H" title="Triple H">Triple H</a> to win the <a href="/wiki/WWE_World_Heavyweight_Championship" title="WWE World Heavyweight Championship">WWE World Heavyweight Championship</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Shane_McMahon" title="Shane McMahon">Shane McMahon</a> (in his first match since 2009) lost to <a href="/wiki/The_Undertaker" title="The Undertaker">The Undertaker</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Hell_in_a_Cell_match" title="Hell in a Cell match" class="mw-redirect">Hell in a Cell match</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brock_Lesnar" title="Brock Lesnar">Brock Lesnar</a> defeated <a href="/wiki/Dean_Ambrose" title="Dean Ambrose">Dean Ambrose</a> in a No Holds Barred Street Fight, and <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Flair" title="Charlotte Flair" class="mw-redirect">Charlotte</a> won the newly-instated <a href="/wiki/WWE_Women%27s_Championship_(2016)" title="WWE Women's Championship (2016)" class="mw-redirect">Women's Championship</a> (which replaced the retired <a href="/wiki/WWE_Divas_Championship" title="WWE Divas Championship">Divas Championship</a>) in a <a href="/wiki/Triple_Threat_match" title="Triple Threat match" class="mw-redirect">Triple Threat match</a> over <a href="/wiki/Becky_Lynch" title="Becky Lynch">Becky Lynch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sasha_Banks" title="Sasha Banks">Sasha Banks</a>.</p> 
     <p>The event was also notable for having the shortest WrestleMania match ever, when <a href="/wiki/Dwayne_Johnson" title="Dwayne Johnson">The Rock</a> beat <a href="/wiki/Erick_Rowan" title="Erick Rowan">Erick Rowan</a> in an impromptu match, the unannounced return of <a href="/wiki/John_Cena" title="John Cena">John Cena</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_the_Giant" title="André the Giant">André the Giant</a> Memorial Battle Royal, which included the surprise participation of <a href="/wiki/NBA" title="NBA" class="mw-redirect">NBA</a> legend <a href="/wiki/Shaquille_O%27Neal" title="Shaquille O'Neal">Shaquille O'Neal</a> and was won by <a href="/wiki/WWE_NXT" title="WWE NXT">NXT</a> call-up <a href="/wiki/Baron_Corbin" title="Baron Corbin">Baron Corbin</a> in his main roster debut. According to WWE<sup id="cite_ref-wwe.com_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wwe.com-7">[7]</a></sup> WrestleMania 32 set multiple records for the company,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> including having grossed $17.3 million and an <a href="#Attendance_and_revenue">official attendance record</a> of 101,763.<sup id="cite_ref-wwe.com_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wwe.com-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-WWErecord_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WWErecord-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WrestleMania_32&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WrestleMania_32&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
     <p><a href="/wiki/WrestleMania" title="WrestleMania">WrestleMania</a> is considered WWE's flagship event,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> having been described as the <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl" title="Super Bowl">Super Bowl</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sports_entertainment" title="Sports entertainment">sports entertainment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The event was the third WrestleMania to be held in the state of Texas, after <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_X-Seven" title="WrestleMania X-Seven">2001</a> and <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_XXV" title="WrestleMania XXV">2009</a>, and the first to take place in the <a href="/wiki/Dallas%E2%80%93Fort_Worth_metroplex" title="DallasFort Worth metroplex">DallasFort Worth metroplex</a> area.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The ticket sales went on dated on November 6, 2015, with individual tickets costing $18 to $1,180.<sup id="cite_ref-32Tickets_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32Tickets-17">[17]</a></sup> On October 13, 2015, traveling packages with accommodation ranging from <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">$</a>575 to $6,625 per person were sold.<sup id="cite_ref-32Tickets_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32Tickets-17">[17]</a></sup> New subscribers to the <a href="/wiki/WWE_Network" title="WWE Network">WWE Network</a> could watch the event at no additional cost.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Dave_Meltzer" title="Dave Meltzer">Dave Meltzer</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/Dave_Meltzer#Wrestling_Observer_Newsletter" title="Dave Meltzer">Wrestling Observer Newsletter</a></i> reported that WrestleMania 32 has broken the WWE's record for most tickets sold with at least 84,000 tickets sold.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i> speculated that WrestleMania 32 could break the WWE attendance record of 93,173 set at <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_III" title="WrestleMania III">WrestleMania III</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Pontiac_Silverdome" title="Pontiac Silverdome">Pontiac Silverdome</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pontiac,_Michigan" title="Pontiac, Michigan">Pontiac, Michigan</a>. This record stood as the highest attendance for any indoor event until the <a href="/wiki/2010_NBA_All-Star_Game" title="2010 NBA All-Star Game">2010 NBA All-Star Game</a>, also held at AT&amp;T Stadium, drew 108,713.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> However, <a href="/wiki/Dave_Meltzer" title="Dave Meltzer">Dave Meltzer</a> reported that the actual WWE attendance record before WrestleMania 32 was the <a href="/wiki/SummerSlam_(1992)" title="SummerSlam (1992)">1992 SummerSlam's</a> 79,127, and that WrestleMania III's actual attendance was around 78,000.<sup id="cite_ref-notinflated1_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-notinflated1-21">[21]</a></sup></p> 
     <p>Five official theme songs were confirmed for the event, they were "<a href="/wiki/My_House_(Flo_Rida_song)" title="My House (Flo Rida song)">My House</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Flo_Rida" title="Flo Rida">Flo Rida</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Hello_Friday" title="Hello Friday">Hello Friday</a>" by Flo Rida featuring <a href="/wiki/Jason_Derulo" title="Jason Derulo">Jason Derulo</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Hail_to_the_King_(song)" title="Hail to the King (song)">Hail to the King</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Avenged_Sevenfold" title="Avenged Sevenfold">Avenged Sevenfold</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Sympathy_for_the_Devil" title="Sympathy for the Devil">Sympathy for the Devil</a>" performed by <a href="/wiki/Mot%C3%B6rhead" title="Motörhead">Motörhead</a> and "Oh No" by Goodbye June. On March 21, it was announced that the American <a href="/wiki/Girl_group" title="Girl group">girl group</a> <a href="/wiki/Fifth_Harmony" title="Fifth Harmony">Fifth Harmony</a> would perform the "America the Beautiful", during the WrestleMania kickoff show.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p> 
     <p>WWE had been hobbled by real-life injuries to the wrestlers on its roster, rendering them unable to wrestle at WrestleMania 32. The injured list at the time included <a href="/wiki/John_Cena" title="John Cena">John Cena</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seth_Rollins" title="Seth Rollins">Seth Rollins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Randy_Orton" title="Randy Orton">Randy Orton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Cesaro" title="Antonio Cesaro">Cesaro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Neville" title="Adrian Neville">Neville</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luke_Harper" title="Luke Harper">Luke Harper</a>. Meanwhile, <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Bryan" title="Daniel Bryan">Daniel Bryan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sting_(wrestler)" title="Sting (wrestler)">Sting</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Bella_Twins" title="The Bella Twins">Nikki Bella</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tyson_Kidd" title="Tyson Kidd">Tyson Kidd</a> suffered neck injuries, with Kidd barely avoiding paralysis or death and Bryan and Sting both retiring from wrestling. David Shoemaker, writing for <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a>, described that "it seems as if the talent left off the match lineup could sell more tickets than the one currently on it." Shoemaker also theorized possible reasons for injuries being firstly "WWE's grueling schedule", and secondly "the travel, the exhaustion, the lack of an <a href="/wiki/Offseason" title="Offseason" class="mw-redirect">offseason</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p> 
     <p>In March 2016, <a href="/wiki/CNET" title="CNET">CNET</a> wrote that WrestleMania 32 would see the culmination of "the biggest story in WWE", which is "establishing <a href="/wiki/Roman_Reigns" title="Roman Reigns">Roman Reigns</a> as the top <a href="/wiki/Face_(professional_wrestling)" title="Face (professional wrestling)">babyface</a>". WWE's attempts to get Reigns the "level of recognition" of name like <a href="/wiki/Hulk_Hogan" title="Hulk Hogan">Hulk Hogan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stone_Cold_Steve_Austin" title="Stone Cold Steve Austin">Stone Cold Steve Austin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dwayne_Johnson" title="Dwayne Johnson">The Rock</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Cena" title="John Cena">John Cena</a> met its "toughest opponent" in the wrestling audience which started a "fan rebellion" in opposition to WWE's support of Reigns, with fans preferring <a href="/wiki/Dean_Ambrose" title="Dean Ambrose">Dean Ambrose</a> instead.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> To combat this trend, WWE has taken measures including muting booing crowds and piping in <a href="/wiki/Laughter_track" title="Laughter track" class="mw-redirect">canned</a> cheers during Reigns' appearances in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_A.V._Club" title="The A.V. Club">The A.V. Club</a></i> commented that "WWE has a serious Roman Reigns problem", due to "basically nobody wanting to see Roman Reigns in the main event" of WrestleMania.<sup id="cite_ref-avclubRR_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-avclubRR-27">[27]</a></sup></p>

     <p>WrestleMania 32 consisted of <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types" title="Professional wrestling match types">professional wrestling matches</a> that involved wrestlers from pre-existing <a href="/wiki/Script_(recorded_media)" title="Script (recorded media)" class="mw-redirect">scripted</a> feuds or <a href="/wiki/Narrative_thread" title="Narrative thread">storylines</a> that played out on WWE television. Wrestlers portrayed heroes or villains as they followed a series of events that built tension and culminated in a wrestling match or series of matches.<sup id="cite_ref-HSW2_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HSW2-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The storylines leading into WrestleMania 32 were not received well by critics. Jason Powell of <i>Pro Wrestling Dot Net</i> lamented that "the build to WrestleMania has been a creative mess", and that WWE's injury-hit roster does not excuse "sloppy booking or storylines with massive holes and logic gaps".<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Jeff Hamlin of the <i>Wrestling Observer Newsletter</i> criticized, "There is no momentum for WrestleMania at all. Club</i> declared that WWE's "ship is headed straight for the rocks", and in particular "the [WWE] World Heavyweight Championship feud is a disaster".<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>

class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Raw3212016-49">[49]</a></sup> followed by <a href="/wiki/Tyler_Breeze" title="Tyler Breeze">Tyler Breeze</a> on the March 25 episode of <i>SmackDown</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Darren_Young" title="Darren Young">Darren Young</a>, <a href="/wiki/Damien_Sandow" title="Damien Sandow">Damien Sandow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fandango_(wrestler)" title="Fandango (wrestler)">Fandango</a>, <a href="/wiki/Goldust" title="Goldust">Goldust</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jack_Swagger" title="Jack Swagger">Jack Swagger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Konnor_(wrestler)" title="Konnor (wrestler)" class="mw-redirect">Konnor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Henry" title="Mark Henry">Mark Henry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ron_Killings" title="Ron Killings">R-Truth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Viktor_(wrestler)" title="Viktor (wrestler)" class="mw-redirect">Viktor</a> on the following episode of <i>Raw</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Raw3282016_50-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Raw3282016-50">[50]</a></sup></p> 
     <p>On the February 8 episode of <i>Raw</i>, after defeating <a href="/wiki/The_New_Day_(wrestling)" title="The New Day (wrestling)">The New Day</a> and Mark Henry in a tag team <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#Tables_match" title="Professional wrestling match types">tables match</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Dudley_Boyz" title="The Dudley Boyz">The Dudley Boyz</a> <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_professional_wrestling_terms#Turn" title="Glossary of professional wrestling terms">turned</a> <a href="/wiki/Heel_(professional_wrestling)" title="Heel (professional wrestling)">heel</a> and betrayed their teammates <a href="/wiki/The_Usos" title="The Usos">The Usos</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Raw2082016_59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Raw2082016-59">[59]</a></sup> Explaining that they did not come back to WWE to be a "nostalgia act" and reminding the fans that they are "the baddest tag team on the planet", The Dudley Boyz abandoned using their trademark tables. In the following weeks, The Usos saved wrestlers from post-match beatdowns by The Dudley Boyz, setting up a tag team match between the two teams for the event. The match was later moved to the <i>WrestleMania 32 Kickoff</i> pre-show.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p> 
     <p>During the '<i>Cutting Edge Peep Show</i> at Fastlane, <a href="/wiki/WWE_Tag_Team_Championship" title="WWE Tag Team Championship">WWE Tag Team Champions</a> The New Day showed signs of a <a href="/wiki/Face_(professional_wrestling)" title="Face (professional wrestling)">face</a> turn by mocking <a href="/wiki/The_League_of_Nations_(professional_wrestling)" title="The League of Nations (professional wrestling)">The League of Nations</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Fastlane2016_43-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fastlane2016-43">[43]</a></sup> Over the next few weeks, The New Day continued to mock The League of Nations in backstage segments. At Roadblock, The New Day retained the WWE Tag Team Championship against League of Nations members Sheamus and <a href="/wiki/Wade_Barrett" title="Wade Barrett">King Barrett</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Roadblock_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Roadblock-47">[47]</a></sup> The New Day then defeated fellow League of Nations members Alberto Del Rio and Rusev the following night on <i>Raw</i> to retain the championship, causing The League of Nations to attack The New Day during the post-match and turning The New Day face,<sup id="cite_ref-Raw3142016_48-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Raw3142016-48">[48]</a></sup> leading to a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_tag_team_match_types#Multiple_man_teamed_matches" title="Professional wrestling tag team match types">six-man tag team match</a> between Sheamus, Alberto Del Rio and Rusev and The New Day at WrestleMania.</p> 
     <p>After walking out on Big Show and Kane during their six-man tag team rematch against The Wyatt Family causing them to lose on February 22 episode of <i>Raw</i>, <a href="/wiki/Ryback" title="Ryback">Ryback</a> became more aggressive by beating his opponents in <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_professional_wrestling_terms#Squash" title="Glossary of professional wrestling terms">squash</a> matches. On March 14 episode of <i>Raw</i>, after defeating <a href="/wiki/Sin_Cara" title="Sin Cara">Sin Cara</a> that night, Ryback challenged <a href="/wiki/WWE_United_States_Championship" title="WWE United States Championship">United States Champion</a> <a href="/wiki/Kalisto_(wrestler)" title="Kalisto (wrestler)">Kalisto</a> to a match for the United States Championship at WrestleMania,<sup id="cite_ref-Raw3142016_48-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Raw3142016-48">[48]</a></sup> which Kalisto accepted two days later in an interview with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Cole_(wrestling)" title="Michael Cole (wrestling)">Michael Cole</a>. The match was later moved to the <i>WrestleMania 32 Kickoff</i> pre-show.</p> 
     <p>After weeks of feuding between each other, <a href="/wiki/The_Miz" title="The Miz">The Miz</a>, Dolph Ziggler, and <a href="/wiki/Sami_Zayn" title="Sami Zayn">Sami Zayn</a> confronted <a href="/wiki/WWE_Intercontinental_Championship" title="WWE Intercontinental Championship">Intercontinental Champion</a> <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Owens" title="Kevin Owens">Kevin Owens</a> on the March 21 episode of <i>Raw</i> and challenged him for the title. Owens agreed to talk to The Authority about setting up a #1 contender triple threat match for a shot at his title, but the match instead included <a href="/wiki/Zack_Ryder" title="Zack Ryder">Zack Ryder</a>, Sin Cara, and <a href="/wiki/Cody_Rhodes" title="Cody Rhodes">Stardust</a>, causing Miz, Ziggler, and Zayn to all interfere in the match, which ended in no contest. Stephanie McMahon then set up a <a href="/wiki/Ladder_match" title="Ladder match">ladder match</a> for the title at WrestleMania, with Owens defending the title against all six competitors.<sup id="cite_ref-Raw3212016_49-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Raw3212016-49">[49]</a></sup></p> 
     <p>After <a href="/wiki/A.J._Styles" title="A.J. Styles">AJ Styles</a> defeated <a href="/wiki/Chris_Jericho" title="Chris Jericho">Chris Jericho</a> at Fastlane,<sup id="cite_ref-Fastlane2016_43-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fastlane2016-43">[43]</a></sup> the two shook hands in respect and formed a <a href="/wiki/Tag_team" title="Tag team">tag team</a> known as "Y2AJ" and dominated the tag team division by defeating champions The New Day in two consecutive matches, leading to Y2AJ challenging them to a match for the WWE Tag Team Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-Raw2222016_44-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Raw2222016-44">[44]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Raw2292016_45-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Raw2292016-45">[45]</a></sup> However, Y2AJ failed to win the championship against The New Day on March 7 episode of <i>Raw</i> after Jericho was pinned by Big E. After the match, Jericho attacked Styles with three <i><a href="/wiki/Facebuster#Double_knee_facebuster" title="Facebuster">Codebreakers</a></i> and shoved their Y2AJ shirt down his mouth, turning heel in the process.<sup id="cite_ref-Raw3072016_61-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Raw3072016-61">[61]</a></sup> Jericho stated that he was tired of the fans chanting for Styles and not him. On the March 21 episode of <i>Raw</i>, during Jericho's match with <a href="/wiki/Fandango_(wrestler)" title="Fandango (wrestler)">Fandango</a>, Styles distracted Jericho by chanting "Y2Jackass" at him, but Jericho won his match despite the distraction.<sup id="cite_ref-Raw3212016_49-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Raw3212016-49">[49]</a></sup> Styles then challenged Jericho to a match at WrestleMania which Jericho refused, stating he would rather sit with the crowd at WrestleMania rather than face him.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> On the March 28 episode of <i>Raw</i>, during Jericho's match with Zack Ryder, Styles once again distracted Jericho by chanting "Y2Jackass" with the crowd while at ringside, this time causing Jericho to lose to Ryder by <a href="/wiki/Pin_(professional_wrestling)#Roll-up" title="Pin (professional wrestling)">roll-up</a>. Angered by this humiliation, Jericho accepted Styles's challenge, but warned him that WrestleMania 32 would be his first and last WrestleMania.<sup id="cite_ref-Raw3282016_50-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Raw3282016-50">[50]</a></sup></p> 
     <p>On the March 14 episode of <i>Raw</i>, <a href="/wiki/Lana_(wrestling)" title="Lana (wrestling)">Lana</a> allied with <a href="/wiki/Team_B.A.D." title="Team B.A.D.">Team B.A.D.</a> (<a href="/wiki/Naomi_Knight" title="Naomi Knight">Naomi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tamina_Snuka" title="Tamina Snuka">Tamina</a>) by helping them defeat Brie Bella and <a href="/wiki/Alicia_Fox" title="Alicia Fox">Alicia Fox</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Raw3142016_48-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Raw3142016-48">[48]</a></sup> Lana then attacked <a href="/wiki/Paige_(wrestler)" title="Paige (wrestler)">Paige</a> after the latter's victory over <a href="/wiki/Summer_Rae" title="Summer Rae">Summer Rae</a> two days later on <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Main_Event" title="WWE Main Event">Main Event</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-ME3162016_63-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ME3162016-63">[63]</a></sup> On the following episode of <i>Main Event</i>, during a match between Paige and Naomi, Rae and <a href="/wiki/Emma_(wrestler)" title="Emma (wrestler)">Emma</a> attacked Fox and <a href="/wiki/Natalya_(wrestler)" title="Natalya (wrestler)">Natalya</a> (in Paige's corner at ringside), allowing Naomi to win.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> On the March 28 episode of <i>Raw</i>, Emma (accompanied by Naomi, Tamina, Lana and Rae) defeated Paige (accompanied by Brie, Fox and Natalya). Following the match, <a href="/wiki/Eva_Marie" title="Eva Marie">Eva Marie</a> allied with Brie, Paige, Fox and Natalya. Subsequently, a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_tag_team_match_types#Multiple_man_teamed_matches" title="Professional wrestling tag team match types">10-Diva tag team match</a> between the two teams was scheduled for the <i>WrestleMania 32 Kickoff</i> pre-show.<sup id="cite_ref-Raw3282016_50-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Raw3282016-50">[50]</a></sup></p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Event">Event</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WrestleMania_32&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Event">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Broadcasters">Broadcasters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WrestleMania_32&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Broadcasters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
     <p>The two-hour <i>WrestleMania Kickoff Show</i> was hosted by <a href="/wiki/Renee_Young" title="Renee Young">Renee Young</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Booker_T_(wrestler)" title="Booker T (wrestler)">Booker T</a>, <a href="/wiki/Corey_Graves" title="Corey Graves">Corey Graves</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lita_(wrestler)" title="Lita (wrestler)">Lita</a> as analysts, while <a href="/wiki/Tom_Phillips_(wrestling)" title="Tom Phillips (wrestling)">Tom Phillips</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cathy_Kelley" title="Cathy Kelley">Cathy Kelley</a> served as <a href="/wiki/Social_media" title="Social media">social media</a> correspondents.</p> 
     <p><a href="/wiki/Michael_Cole_(wrestling)" title="Michael Cole (wrestling)">Michael Cole</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Layfield" title="John Layfield">John "Bradshaw" Layfield</a> (main show), <a href="/wiki/Mauro_Ranallo" title="Mauro Ranallo">Mauro Ranallo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Lawler" title="Jerry Lawler">Jerry "The King" Lawler</a> (pre-show), and <a href="/wiki/Byron_Saxton" title="Byron Saxton">Byron Saxton</a> (all matches) served as the English commentators, while Spanish, German, French, Italian, Russian, Portuguese and Japanese commentators also called the matches live at the stadium. <a href="/wiki/Lilian_Garcia" title="Lilian Garcia">Lilian Garcia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eden_Stiles" title="Eden Stiles">Eden Stiles</a> served as the ring announcers.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Celebrities">Celebrities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WrestleMania_32&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Celebrities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
     <p>As is tradition at WrestleMania, the show included appearances by numerous celebrity guests. <a href="/wiki/Fifth_Harmony" title="Fifth Harmony">Fifth Harmony</a> performed "<a href="/wiki/America_the_Beautiful" title="America the Beautiful">America the Beautiful</a>" at the start of the PPV broadcast, <i><a href="/wiki/E!_News" title="E! News">E! News</a></i> host <a href="/wiki/Maria_Menounos" title="Maria Menounos">Maria Menounos</a> served as a backstage interviewer, and <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a> - who the night before was inducted into the celebrity wing of the <a href="/wiki/WWE_Hall_of_Fame" title="WWE Hall of Fame">WWE Hall of Fame</a> - performed a live version of his cousin <a href="/wiki/Sasha_Banks" title="Sasha Banks">Sasha Banks</a>' entrance theme as he accompanied her to the ring for her match.</p>

     <p>Former long time <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_America" title="Good Morning America">Good Morning America</a></i> co-host <a href="/wiki/Joan_Lunden" title="Joan Lunden">Joan Lunden</a> was recognized as part of the 2016 WWE Hall of Fame class, as she won the <a href="/wiki/The_Ultimate_Warrior" title="The Ultimate Warrior">Warrior Award</a> for her public battle with <a href="/wiki/Breast_cancer" title="Breast cancer">breast cancer</a>. <a href="/wiki/NBA" title="NBA" class="mw-redirect">NBA</a> great <a href="/wiki/Shaquille_O%27Neal" title="Shaquille O'Neal">Shaquille O'Neal</a> was a surprise participant in the André the Giant Memorial Battle Royal (eliminating <a href="/wiki/Damien_Sandow" title="Damien Sandow">Damien Sandow</a> and having a standoff with fellow seven-footer <a href="/wiki/Big_Show" title="Big Show">Big Show</a> before being eliminated himself by about 10 other wrestlers), and the <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Cowboys_Cheerleaders" title="Dallas Cowboys Cheerleaders">Dallas Cowboys Cheerleaders</a> performed on stage as part of <a href="/wiki/Dwayne_Johnson" title="Dwayne Johnson">The Rock's</a> grand entrance.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-show">Pre-show</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WrestleMania_32&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Pre-show">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
     <p>Three matches were contested on the two-hour long <i>WrestleMania Kickoff</i> pre-show, which was broadcast on the WWE Network, WWE.com, <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a>, <a href="/wiki/Google_%2B" title="Google +" class="mw-redirect">Google +</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pintrest" title="Pintrest" class="mw-redirect">Pintrest</a> and <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>, with <a href="/wiki/USA_Network" title="USA Network">USA Network</a> joining the live coverage for the second hour.</p> 
     <p>In the first hour, <a href="/wiki/Kalisto_(wrestler)" title="Kalisto (wrestler)">Kalisto</a> defended his <a href="/wiki/WWE_United_States_Championship" title="WWE United States Championship">WWE United States Championship</a> against <a href="/wiki/Ryback" title="Ryback">Ryback</a>. While Ryback used his size advantage to control most of the match, Kalisto won after executing a Salida del Sol on Ryback after he bumped his head on an exposed turnbuckle.</p> 
     <p>In the second hour, a 10-Diva tag team match pitted <a href="/wiki/Total_Divas" title="Total Divas">Team Total Divas</a> against <a href="/wiki/Team_B.A.D." title="Team B.A.D.">Team B.A.D. &amp; Blonde</a>. <a href="/wiki/Brie_Bella" title="Brie Bella" class="mw-redirect">Brie Bella</a> won the match for Team Total Divas by making <a href="/wiki/Naomi_Knight" title="Naomi Knight">Naomi</a> submit to a Yes! lock. <a href="/wiki/Nikki_Bella" title="Nikki Bella" class="mw-redirect">Nikki Bella</a>, who is still inactive due to a neck injury, came out to celebrate with her sister and the rest of the team. Next, <a href="/wiki/WWE_Hall_of_Fame" title="WWE Hall of Fame">WWE Hall of Famer</a> <a href="/wiki/Lita_(wrestler)" title="Lita (wrestler)">Lita</a> unveiled the newly-established <a href="/wiki/WWE_Women%27s_Championship_(2016%E2%80%93present)" title="WWE Women's Championship (2016present)">WWE Women's Championship</a>, which would replace the <a href="/wiki/WWE_Divas_Championship" title="WWE Divas Championship">WWE Divas Championship</a>. Lita announced that the winner of the triple threat match between <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_(wrestler)" title="Charlotte (wrestler)">Charlotte</a>, <a href="/wiki/Becky_Lynch" title="Becky Lynch">Becky Lynch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sasha_Banks" title="Sasha Banks">Sasha Banks</a> later that night would be awarded the title. Finally, <a href="/wiki/The_Usos" title="The Usos">The Usos</a> defeated <a href="/wiki/The_Dudley_Boyz" title="The Dudley Boyz">The Dudley Boyz</a> in a tag team match. After the match, The Usos frog splashed the Dudleys through tables.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_card">Main card</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WrestleMania_32&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Main card">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
     <p>The main card broadcast opened with a seven-man <a href="/wiki/Ladder_match" title="Ladder match">ladder match</a> for <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Owens" title="Kevin Owens">Kevin Owens</a>' <a href="/wiki/WWE_Intercontinental_Championship" title="WWE Intercontinental Championship">WWE Intercontinental Championship</a>. Following over 15 minutes of wild action (including <a href="/wiki/Hunico" title="Hunico">Sin Cara</a> being knocked off a ladder by Owens and smashing a prone <a href="/wiki/Cody_Rhodes" title="Cody Rhodes">Stardust</a> through a ladder that was laying across the ring and security barricade) <a href="/wiki/The_Miz" title="The Miz">The Miz</a> ascended a ladder in the climax of the match when he was shoved off at the last second by <a href="/wiki/Zack_Ryder" title="Zack Ryder">Zack Ryder</a>, who then grabbed the belt to win the match. Ryder celebrated his win by inviting his father (who was sitting in the audience at ringside) into the ring.</p> 
     <p>Next, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Jericho" title="Chris Jericho">Chris Jericho</a> took on <a href="/wiki/A.J._Styles" title="A.J. Styles">AJ Styles</a>. After an evenly contested battle (which saw Styles kick out of Jericho's Codebreaker finisher, and Jericho kick out of Styles' Styles Clash), Jericho countered Styles' Phenomenal Forearm into a mid-air Codebreaker to score the pinfall victory.</p> 
     <p>Zack Ryder was interviewed backstage by Maria Menounos regarding his Intercontinental Championship victory earlier in the night. Ryder stated that he "lived his dream to become champion".</p>

The League of Nations won the match following outside inference by Barrett when the referee was distracted. Afterwards, Barrett bragged that no three men could beat the League of Nations. <a href="/wiki/WWE_Hall_of_Fame" title="WWE Hall of Fame">WWE Hall of Famers</a> <a href="/wiki/Shawn_Michaels" title="Shawn Michaels">Shawn Michaels</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mick_Foley" title="Mick Foley">Mick Foley</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Stone_Cold_Steve_Austin" title="Stone Cold Steve Austin">Stone Cold Steve Austin</a>, then came down and cleared the ring of Barrett and his cohorts. Austin then delivered a Stunner to Woods, who had tried to persuade the Hall of Famers to dance with him.</p> 
     <p>The No Holds Barred Street Fight between <a href="/wiki/Brock_Lesnar" title="Brock Lesnar">Brock Lesnar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dean_Ambrose" title="Dean Ambrose">Dean Ambrose</a> was next. The match saw Lesnar executed a total of thirteen suplexes on Ambrose, while Ambrose used kendo sticks and chairs to try to keep Lesnar down (Ambrose also attempted to use a chainsaw, which didn't start). After Ambrose threw numerous chairs in the ring, he brought in a barbed wire wrapped baseball bat to finish Lesnar off, but Lesnar caught Ambrose with a suplex, and executed an F5 finishing move on the pile of chairs to win the match. Afterwards, Lesnar sprayed the fire extinguisher on Ambrose.</p> 
     <p>Following the <a href="/wiki/WWE_Hall_of_Fame#2016" title="WWE Hall of Fame">2016 WWE Hall of Fame</a> class being recognised, <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_(wrestler)" title="Charlotte (wrestler)">Charlotte</a>, <a href="/wiki/Becky_Lynch" title="Becky Lynch">Becky Lynch</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sasha_Banks" title="Sasha Banks">Sasha Banks</a> (who had her cousin and newly-minted WWE Hall of Famer <a href="/wiki/Snoop_Dogg" title="Snoop Dogg">Snoop Dogg</a> perform her entrance theme while accompanying her to the ring) wrestled in a Triple Threat match. Originally promoted as being for Charlotte's <a href="/wiki/WWE_Divas_Championship" title="WWE Divas Championship">WWE Divas Championship</a>, the match would instead determine the new <a href="/wiki/WWE_Women%27s_Championship_(2016)" title="WWE Women's Championship (2016)" class="mw-redirect">WWE Women's Champion</a>, as the Divas title would be retired. The ending of the match saw Charlotte use her Figure-Eight leg lock on Lynch, while <a href="/wiki/Ric_Flair" title="Ric Flair">Ric Flair</a> (Charlotte's manager and father) prevented Banks from getting in the ring and intervening. Lynch submitted, giving the win and the WWE Women's title to Charlotte.</p> 
     <p>The <a href="/wiki/Hell_in_a_Cell_match" title="Hell in a Cell match" class="mw-redirect">Hell in a Cell match</a> between <a href="/wiki/The_Undertaker" title="The Undertaker">The Undertaker</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shane_McMahon" title="Shane McMahon">Shane McMahon</a> was next. If McMahon won, he would get control of <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw" title="WWE Raw">Raw</a></i>, while Undertaker would be barred from wrestling at future WrestleManias. Though Undertaker dominated the bulk of the match, McMahon would keep fighting, finally getting an advantage by reversing Undertaker's Hell's Gate submission move into a Sharpshooter. McMahon then placed a garbage can in front of Undertaker's face and hit his Coast-to-Coast finishing move, which Undertaker kicked out of at two. Undertaker then slammed McMahon through the side of the cell, and the two fought on the floor around ringside. After Undertaker slammed McMahon through a broadcast table to get out of a sleeper hold, McMahon recovered and struck Undertaker in the head with TV monitors and a metal toolbox to keep him down, and placed him across another broadcast table. Shane then climbed all the way up to the top of the 20 foot tall cell, and attempted to hit an elbow drop onto Undertaker, who moved out of the way at the last second, causing McMahon to crash trough the table from 20 feet in the air. Though McMahon would motion for Undertaker to "bring it", he had no strength left to fight, and Undertaker would deliver a Tombstone piledriver and pinned McMahon to win the match. McMahon wound up being carted out of the stadium on a stretcher (while showing a thumbs-up to the crowd) while Undertaker walked on his own two feet and smiled.</p> 
     <p>Next was the <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_the_Giant" title="André the Giant">André the Giant</a> Memorial Battle Royal. There were several surprise entrants in the match, including <a href="/wiki/WWE_Legends" title="WWE Legends" class="mw-redirect">legends</a> <a href="/wiki/Diamond_Dallas_Page" title="Diamond Dallas Page">Diamond Dallas Page</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tatanka" title="Tatanka" class="mw-disambig">Tatanka</a>, <a href="/wiki/WWE_NXT" title="WWE NXT">NXT</a> call-up <a href="/wiki/Baron_Corbin" title="Baron Corbin">Baron Corbin</a> making his main roster debut, and <a href="/wiki/NBA" title="NBA" class="mw-redirect">NBA</a> legend <a href="/wiki/Shaquille_O%27Neal" title="Shaquille O'Neal">Shaquille O'Neal</a> as a celebrity entry. O'Neal and <a href="/wiki/Big_Show" title="Big Show">Big Show</a> double chokeslammed <a href="/wiki/Kane_(wrestler)" title="Kane (wrestler)">Kane</a> to start, and the other wrestlers were then thrown aside (though not eliminated) by the two seven-footers. After <a href="/wiki/Fandango_(wrestler)" title="Fandango (wrestler)">Fandango</a> and <a href="/wiki/Damien_Sandow" title="Damien Sandow">Damien Sandow</a> were eliminated by Show and O'Neal respectively, the two big men fought to the ropes, where the rest of the field ganged up and eliminated them both. In the end, Corbin won the match, last eliminating Kane.</p>

     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_event">Main event</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WrestleMania_32&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Main event">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
     <p>The main event match saw <a href="/wiki/Triple_H" title="Triple H">Triple H</a> defend his <a href="/wiki/WWE_World_Heavyweight_Championship" title="WWE World Heavyweight Championship">WWE World Heavyweight Championship</a> against <a href="/wiki/Roman_Reigns" title="Roman Reigns">Roman Reigns</a>. Triple H made a <i><a href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones" title="Game of Thrones">Game of Thrones</a></i> inspired grand entrance, including his wife/manager <a href="/wiki/Stephanie_McMahon" title="Stephanie McMahon">Stephanie McMahon</a> declaring herself as the "Queen of Queens". Reigns made his entrance by using his Superman punch on the floor followed by fireworks being set off in and around the exterior of the stadium.</p> 
     <p>After a fast start by Reigns, Triple H methodically worked over Reigns' left arm. Reigns then rallied by spearing Triple H through the ringside barrier. Triple H recovered and again went after the arm, but Reigns rallied again, hitting another spear on Triple H, but Stephanie pulled out the referee (Chad Patton) after getting to two on the pinfall attempt. Stephanie entered the ring to argue with the referee, when she was inadvertently struck by Reigns' spear, as he was aiming for Triple H who had sidestepped the oncoming Reigns. Triple H executed a Pedigree on Reigns, but secured a two count. Triple H, in disbelief, crawled to Stephanie (who was still suffering the effects of Reigns' spear) who handed him his trademark sledgehammer. The referee warned Triple H that he would get disqualified if he used it, then Reigns hit two Superman Punches. Reigns then ran the ropes, dodged a sledgehammer shot, and responded with a third spear to garner the pinfall victory and win the title for a third time. A wide array of celebratory fireworks was released.</p> 
     <p>As predicted by multiple critics before the event,<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> Reigns (despite playing a heroic character) was booed by the WrestleMania crowd during the main event and as the show went off the air with him as champion,<sup id="cite_ref-observerreport_67-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-observerreport-67">[67]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rollingstonereport_72-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rollingstonereport-72">[72]</a></sup> with the result drawing "bullshit" chants. To placate the angry crowd, WWE had Triple H play to the crowd after broadcasting had concluded.<sup id="cite_ref-rollingstonereport_72-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rollingstonereport-72">[72]</a></sup></p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WrestleMania_32&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

     <p>WWE claimed an attendance of 101,763<sup id="cite_ref-WWErecord_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WWErecord-13">[13]</a></sup> in the AT&amp;T Stadium, which surpassed the previous WWE record, a claimed attendance of 93,173 for <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_III" title="WrestleMania III">WrestleMania III</a> in 1987. Also, WWE reported the event grossing $17.3 million for WWE's highest grossing live event.<sup id="cite_ref-WWErecord_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WWErecord-13">[13]</a></sup> However, journalist <a href="/wiki/Dave_Meltzer" title="Dave Meltzer">Dave Meltzer</a> reported 93,370 as "the attendance as would be normally announced for an event" with a total 97,769 people in the stadium if one includes stadium personnel, WWE personnel, etc.<sup id="cite_ref-notinflated1_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-notinflated1-21">[21]</a></sup> He endorsed the grossing figure as "either accurate or very close".<sup id="cite_ref-notinflated2_73-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-notinflated2-73">[73]</a></sup></p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reviews">Reviews</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WrestleMania_32&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Reviews">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
     <p>WrestleMania 32 received mixed reactions<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> from critics, who criticized the event for its length and questionable booking decisions. Critics who reviewed both WrestleMania 32 and WWE's <a href="/wiki/NXT_TakeOver:_Dallas" title="NXT TakeOver: Dallas">NXT TakeOver: Dallas</a> event which occured two days earlier concluded that the NXT event was a greatly superior "professional wrestling show" despite WrestleMania having more "spectacle"; also NXT's match of (the debuting) <a href="/wiki/Shinsuke_Nakamura" title="Shinsuke Nakamura">Shinsuke Nakamura</a> versus Sami Zayn was praised as better than any moment or match from WrestleMania 32.<sup id="cite_ref-allhail_77-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allhail-77">[77]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nakamegastar_79-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nakamegastar-79">[79]</a></sup></p> 
     <p>Troy L. Smith of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Plain_Dealer" title="The Plain Dealer">cleveland.com</a></i> wrote that "WrestleMania 32 was a night that defied low expectations, thanks in large part a ton of WWE nostalgia", but added that "some of that early goodwill was squandered by odd booking decisions". Smith felt that the main event was "extremely boring  one of the worst main events ever at WrestleMania", with the ending saw "WWE found itself stuck in a moment it couldn't get out of with more boos than cheers". Ultimately, Smith concluded that "WrestleMania 32 proves Zack Ryder (or anyone) is better than Roman Reigns".<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup></p> 
     <p>Paul Tamburro of <a href="/wiki/CraveOnline" title="CraveOnline">CraveOnline</a> wrote that the show was characterized by "inexplicable booking decisions that actively undermined all storyline progression", adding that "WWE routinely swerved its fans with finishes to matches that were less exciting than what had been expected". He decried the main event as a "dull, slow-paced match" and described Reigns' babyface <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_professional_wrestling_terms#Push" title="Glossary of professional wrestling terms">push</a> as "absurd" and "an embarrassing failed experiment for the company", with "the crowd still being given no reason to feel anything for the guy outside of sheer ambivalence or venomous hatred". Tamburro was also critical of the Shane McMahonUndertaker match, which he described as "thoroughly pointless", and of the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_professional_wrestling_terms#Book" title="Glossary of professional wrestling terms">booking</a> of Dean Ambrose, writing that "him losing to Lesnar in such a disappointing manner puts a big dent in his value". He had further criticism for the company's over-reliance on past stars, writing that "while the implementation of these big names was a lot of fun, it still happened to the detriment of talent currently on the roster". In contrast, Tamburro praised the Women's Championship match, calling it "triumphant", and described Zack Ryder's unexpected win as "the feel-good moment of the show".<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup></p> 
     <p>James Caldwell of <i>Pro Wrestling Torch</i> described the event as a "weird show". He rated the main event match 1.5 stars out of 5, questioning why "anyone wanted to sit through this long of a main event" and criticizing "the egos of the McMahon family" for booking the match. Caldwell went on to describe Reigns as "a pet project that would be laughed out of this spot in any other era". Caldwell chose not to rate the Hell in a Cell match, describing it as a "weird deal" with "Shane McMahon, a non-wrestler against Undertaker in one of Takers last WrestleMania matches". The women's title match was rated 3 stars, "strong" other than the finish, which puzzled Caldwell when apparently "Mania isn't a big enough event to pay off Flair's repeated interference with the face(s) conquering".<sup id="cite_ref-WrestleMania32_82-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WrestleMania32-82">[82]</a></sup></p> 
     <p>Nolan Howell of the <i><a href="/wiki/Canoe.ca" title="Canoe.ca">Canadian Online Explorer</a></i> described the show as "absurdly bizarre. Strange booking decisions and nothing seemed to be resolved at all, aside from the impromptu Shane vs. Undertaker story which could have been tied up any time". Howell wrote that while "the match quality was fine", the show was "spectacle without a lot of substance". He described the second half of the show as a "train wreck creatively", adding that "WWE just can't do anything right really". Howell described the Women's Championship match as the match of the night, and for the main event commented on the "most predictable" boos for Roman Reigns, with the crowd microphones "suspiciously" going largely silent several times during and after the main event.<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup> Dave Scherer of <i>Pro Wrestling Insider</i> commented on responses that WrestleMania 32 was "great", countering that "unless you are putting the framework in place for the future, you will reach a point where most of your assets are gone and you will be in a barren place". This was referencing that "the finishes and character development (or lack thereof)" resulting in stars of the past (Austin, Foley, Michaels, Jericho, Rock, Cena) shining at the expense of current wrestlers (Styles, The New Day, The League of Nations and The Wyatt Family). Scherer also criticized Vince McMahon for ignoring the "clear edict" from fans against Roman Reigns as world champion.<sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84">[84]</a></sup></p> 
     <p>Scott Keith of <a href="/wiki/Sporting_News" title="Sporting News">Sporting News</a> wrote that WrestleMania 32 "ended up being a five-hour slog (seven hours with pregame show) and one of the worst WrestleManias when all was said and done". He wrote that "Roman Reigns showed once again that he's not the guy to be the top star despite Triple H's best efforts to get a 'classic' match out of him, and the ShaneUndertaker match ended up being a 30-minute snoozefest instead of the chaotic mess that everyone wanted". Keith praised the Women's Championship match and Zack Ryder for having his "WrestleMania moment".<sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup></p> 
     <p>Aaron Oster of <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> wrote that "fans witnessed a roller-coaster ride that brought the WWE Universe from the highest of highs to the lowest of lows. Before looking at the bad, it's worth pointing out that there was also a lot of good". However, Oster felt that while WrestleMania 31 tried to "cater to disgruntled fans", WrestleMania 32 "felt like a six-hour attempt to piss off that same demo", especially the event's ending, which caused fans to feel "betrayed" after "dream endings" at <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_30" title="WrestleMania 30" class="mw-redirect">WrestleMania 30</a> and 31.<sup id="cite_ref-rollingstonereport_72-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rollingstonereport-72">[72]</a></sup></p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WrestleMania_32&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <p>On the first <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw" title="WWE Raw">Raw</a></i> after WrestleMania from <a href="/wiki/American_Airlines_Center" title="American Airlines Center">American Airlines Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dallas,_Texas" title="Dallas, Texas" class="mw-redirect">Dallas, Texas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vince_McMahon" title="Vince McMahon">Mr. McMahon</a> returned to confront the crowd before being interrupted by <a href="/wiki/Shane_McMahon" title="Shane McMahon">Shane McMahon</a>, who offered a handshake to his father following his loss the previous night, and went to depart the arena. Vince, saying that "nobody upstages [him]" in reference to Shane receiving adoration from the live audience, granted Shane control of <i>Raw</i> for the evening. Shane granted <a href="/wiki/Uhaa_Nation" title="Uhaa Nation" class="mw-redirect">Apollo Crews</a> his main roster debut, where he defeated <a href="/wiki/Tyler_Breeze" title="Tyler Breeze">Tyler Breeze</a>. The night also saw the main roster debut of <a href="/wiki/Baron_Corbin" title="Baron Corbin">Baron Corbin</a>, who faced <a href="/wiki/Dolph_Ziggler" title="Dolph Ziggler">Dolph Ziggler</a>. The match ended in a draw due to a double countout after Corbin sent Ziggler into the live crowd. He proceeded to deliver the <i>End of Days</i> to Ziggler, following a beatdown.</p> 
     <p><a href="/wiki/The_New_Day_(wrestling)" title="The New Day (wrestling)">The New Day</a> defended their <a href="/wiki/WWE_Tag_Team_Championship" title="WWE Tag Team Championship">WWE Tag Team Championship</a> against <a href="/wiki/Sheamus" title="Sheamus">Sheamus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wade_Barrett" title="Wade Barrett">King Barrett</a> of <a href="/wiki/The_League_of_Nations_(professional_wrestling)" title="The League of Nations (professional wrestling)">The League of Nations</a>. The New Day retained the titles after Kingston gained the pinfall over Barrett. Angered by the loss, the group turned on Barrett, labelling him the group's "weakest link", but were subsequently attacked by <a href="/wiki/The_Wyatt_Family" title="The Wyatt Family">The Wyatt Family</a> (<a href="/wiki/Bray_Wyatt" title="Bray Wyatt">Bray Wyatt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Erick_Rowan" title="Erick Rowan">Erick Rowan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Braun_Strowman" title="Braun Strowman">Braun Strowman</a>).</p> 
     <p>On April 11 episode of Raw, Shane McMahon announced a tag team tournament to decide the number one contender to The New Day's Tag Team Championships. <a href="/wiki/The_Dudley_Boyz" title="The Dudley Boyz">The Dudley Boyz</a> advanced by defeating <a href="/wiki/The_Lucha_Dragons" title="The Lucha Dragons">The Lucha Dragons</a> while <a href="/wiki/The_Usos" title="The Usos">The Usos</a> defeated <a href="/wiki/The_Social_Outcasts" title="The Social Outcasts">The Social Outcasts</a> to advance. After the match, The Usos were attacked by the debuting <a href="/wiki/Doc_Gallows" title="Doc Gallows">Luke Gallows</a> and <a href="/wiki/Karl_Anderson" title="Karl Anderson">Karl Anderson</a> who are recognized as two members of the <a href="/wiki/Bullet_Club" title="Bullet Club">Bullet Club</a> stable in Japan.</p> 
     <p>Despite winning his first <a href="/wiki/WWE_Intercontinental_Championship" title="WWE Intercontinental Championship">Intercontinental Championship</a> at WrestleMania, <a href="/wiki/Zack_Ryder" title="Zack Ryder">Zack Ryder</a> lost the title to <a href="/wiki/The_Miz" title="The Miz">The Miz</a> after an argument between Ryder's father, and Miz's wife, <a href="/wiki/Maryse_Ouellet" title="Maryse Ouellet">Maryse</a>. The argument served as a distraction to Ryder, allowing Miz to hit him with the <i><a href="/wiki/Facebuster#Full_nelson_facebuster" title="Facebuster">Skull-Crushing Finale</a></i>, and win the title for a fifth time.</p> 
     <p>A ceremony to welcome the newly introduced <a href="/wiki/WWE_Women%27s_Championship_(2016%E2%80%93present)" title="WWE Women's Championship (2016present)">WWE Women's Championship</a> followed, with every female wrestler on the main roster present inside the ring. After appearing to congratulate <a href="/wiki/Sasha_Banks" title="Sasha Banks">Sasha Banks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Becky_Lynch" title="Becky Lynch">Becky Lynch</a> following their match at WrestleMania, <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_(wrestler)" title="Charlotte (wrestler)">Charlotte</a> began to brag about her success the night before. Feeling disrespected, the rest of the women's roster left the ceremony, with <a href="/wiki/Natalya_(wrestler)" title="Natalya (wrestler)">Natalya</a> staying behind and confronted Charlotte about the need for humility. After Charlotte disrespected the <a href="/wiki/Hart_wrestling_family" title="Hart wrestling family">Hart family</a>, Natalya attacked the champion and locked her in her signature move, the <i>Sharpshooter</i> until Ric Flair pulled his daughter out of the ring.</p> 
     <p>A rematch of their encounter at WrestleMania between The Usos and The Dudley Boyz commenced in a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#Tables_match" title="Professional wrestling match types">tables match</a>, with The Dudley Boyz winning the reverse fixture. Soon after, <a href="/wiki/Enzo_Amore_and_Colin_Cassady" title="Enzo Amore and Colin Cassady">Enzo Amore and Colin Cassady</a> appeared, confronting the The Dudley Boyz, who had left following an altercation.</p> 
     <p>After winning the <a href="/wiki/WWE_World_Heavyweight_Championship" title="WWE World Heavyweight Championship">WWE World Heavyweight Championship</a> from <a href="/wiki/Triple_H" title="Triple H">Triple H</a> the previous night, <a href="/wiki/Roman_Reigns" title="Roman Reigns">Roman Reigns</a> issued an open challenge to anyone in the locker room for a shot at the title. After <a href="/wiki/Chris_Jericho" title="Chris Jericho">Chris Jericho</a>, <a href="/wiki/A.J._Styles" title="A.J. Styles">AJ Styles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Owens" title="Kevin Owens">Kevin Owens</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sami_Zayn" title="Sami Zayn">Sami Zayn</a> all voiced their interest at a potential title shot, Shane scheduled a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#Basic_non-elimination_matches" title="Professional wrestling match types">fatal four-way match</a> between Jericho, Styles, Owens and Zayn to determine the #1 contender for Reigns' title. During a backstage interview, Zayn was attacked by Owens, who hit Zayn with a <a href="/wiki/Powerbomb" title="Powerbomb">powerbomb</a> through a table, rendering him unable to compete in the match. Zayn was then replaced with a returning <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Cesaro" title="Antonio Cesaro">Cesaro</a> in the four-way match. Styles won the match, pinning Jericho after a <i><a href="/wiki/Facebuster#Belly-to-back_inverted_mat_slam" title="Facebuster">Styles Clash</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-86" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup></p> 
     <p>After helping Total Divas win their match at WrestleMania, Brie Bella announced that she was taking an extended break from wrestling on April 6, 2016, so that she could focus on starting a family with her retired husband <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Bryan" title="Daniel Bryan">Daniel Bryan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-87" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup></p>
