WrestleMania 32 was the thirty-second annual WrestleMania professional wrestling pay-per-view (PPV) event produced by WWE. It took place on April 3, 2016, at AT&T Stadium in Arlington, Texas.[8] Twelve matches were contested (with three matches occurring on the WrestleMania Kickoff pre-show - two airing live on the USA Network, which televised the second hour of the pre-show).

Four matches were considered marquee attractions:[9][10][11] In the main event, Roman Reigns defeated Triple H to win the WWE World Heavyweight Championship, while Shane McMahon (in his first match since 2009) lost to The Undertaker in a Hell in a Cell match, Brock Lesnar defeated Dean Ambrose in a No Holds Barred Street Fight, and Charlotte won the newly-instated Women's Championship (which replaced the retired Divas Championship) in a Triple Threat match over Becky Lynch and Sasha Banks.

The event was also notable for having the shortest WrestleMania match ever, when The Rock beat Erick Rowan in an impromptu match, the unannounced return of John Cena, and the André the Giant Memorial Battle Royal, which included the surprise participation of NBA legend Shaquille O'Neal and was won by NXT call-up Baron Corbin in his main roster debut. According to WWE[7] WrestleMania 32 set multiple records for the company,[12] including having grossed $17.3 million and an official attendance record of 101,763.[7][13]

Production[edit]

Background[edit]

WrestleMania is considered WWE's flagship event,[14] having been described as the Super Bowl of sports entertainment.[15] The event was the third WrestleMania to be held in the state of Texas, after 2001 and 2009, and the first to take place in the DallasFort Worth metroplex area.[16] The ticket sales went on dated on November 6, 2015, with individual tickets costing $18 to $1,180.[17] On October 13, 2015, traveling packages with accommodation ranging from $575 to $6,625 per person were sold.[17] New subscribers to the WWE Network could watch the event at no additional cost.[18] Dave Meltzer of the Wrestling Observer Newsletter reported that WrestleMania 32 has broken the WWE's record for most tickets sold with at least 84,000 tickets sold.[19] Forbes speculated that WrestleMania 32 could break the WWE attendance record of 93,173 set at WrestleMania III at the Pontiac Silverdome in Pontiac, Michigan. This record stood as the highest attendance for any indoor event until the 2010 NBA All-Star Game, also held at AT&T Stadium, drew 108,713.[20] However, Dave Meltzer reported that the actual WWE attendance record before WrestleMania 32 was the 1992 SummerSlam's 79,127, and that WrestleMania III's actual attendance was around 78,000.[21]

Five official theme songs were confirmed for the event, they were "My House" by Flo Rida, "Hello Friday" by Flo Rida featuring Jason Derulo, "Hail to the King" by Avenged Sevenfold, "Sympathy for the Devil" performed by Motörhead and "Oh No" by Goodbye June. On March 21, it was announced that the American girl group Fifth Harmony would perform the "America the Beautiful", during the WrestleMania kickoff show.[22]

WWE had been hobbled by real-life injuries to the wrestlers on its roster, rendering them unable to wrestle at WrestleMania 32. The injured list at the time included John Cena, Seth Rollins, Randy Orton, Cesaro, Neville and Luke Harper. Meanwhile, Daniel Bryan, Sting, Nikki Bella and Tyson Kidd suffered neck injuries, with Kidd barely avoiding paralysis or death and Bryan and Sting both retiring from wrestling. David Shoemaker, writing for ESPN, described that "it seems as if the talent left off the match lineup could sell more tickets than the one currently on it." Shoemaker also theorized possible reasons for injuries being firstly "WWE's grueling schedule", and secondly "the travel, the exhaustion, the lack of an offseason".[23]

In March 2016, CNET wrote that WrestleMania 32 would see the culmination of "the biggest story in WWE", which is "establishing Roman Reigns as the top babyface". WWE's attempts to get Reigns the "level of recognition" of name like Hulk Hogan, Stone Cold Steve Austin, The Rock and John Cena met its "toughest opponent" in the wrestling audience which started a "fan rebellion" in opposition to WWE's support of Reigns, with fans preferring Dean Ambrose instead.[24] To combat this trend, WWE has taken measures including muting booing crowds and piping in canned cheers during Reigns' appearances in 2016.[25][26] The A.V. Club commented that "WWE has a serious Roman Reigns problem", due to "basically nobody wanting to see Roman Reigns in the main event" of WrestleMania.[27]

Storylines[edit]

See also: Professional wrestling

WrestleMania 32 consisted of professional wrestling matches that involved wrestlers from pre-existing scripted feuds or storylines that played out on WWE television. Wrestlers portrayed heroes or villains as they followed a series of events that built tension and culminated in a wrestling match or series of matches.[28][29] The storylines leading into WrestleMania 32 were not received well by critics. Jason Powell of Pro Wrestling Dot Net lamented that "the build to WrestleMania has been a creative mess", and that WWE's injury-hit roster does not excuse "sloppy booking or storylines with massive holes and logic gaps".[30] Jeff Hamlin of the Wrestling Observer Newsletter criticized, "There is no momentum for WrestleMania at all. The event sells itself purely on its brand name".[31] Ben Tucker of Pro Wrestling Torch wrote, "Not since WrestleMania 13 has WWE's Super Bowl come together in such a bizarre fashion, with WWE making some of the most head-scratching decisions I've seen in ages".[32] Kyle Fowle of The A.V. Club declared that WWE's "ship is headed straight for the rocks", and in particular "the [WWE] World Heavyweight Championship feud is a disaster".[33]

On the February 22 episode of Raw, Mr. McMahon presented the inaugural "Vincent J. McMahon Legacy of Excellence" Award to his daughter Stephanie McMahon. Event[edit]

Broadcasters[edit]

The two-hour WrestleMania Kickoff Show was hosted by Renee Young, with Booker T, Corey Graves, and Lita as analysts, while Tom Phillips and Cathy Kelley served as social media correspondents.

Michael Cole and John "Bradshaw" Layfield (main show), Mauro Ranallo and Jerry "The King" Lawler (pre-show), and Byron Saxton (all matches) served as the English commentators, while Spanish, German, French, Italian, Russian, Portuguese and Japanese commentators also called the matches live at the stadium. Lilian Garcia and Eden Stiles served as the ring announcers.

Celebrities[edit]

As is tradition at WrestleMania, the show included appearances by numerous celebrity guests. Fifth Harmony performed "America the Beautiful" at the start of the PPV broadcast, E! News host Maria Menounos served as a backstage interviewer, and Snoop Dogg - who the night before was inducted into the celebrity wing of the WWE Hall of Fame - performed a live version of his cousin Sasha Banks' entrance theme as he accompanied her to the ring for her match.

NBA legend Shaquille O'Neal was a surprise participant in the André the Giant Memorial Battle Royal

Former long time Good Morning America co-host Joan Lunden was recognized as part of the 2016 WWE Hall of Fame class, as she won the Warrior Award for her public battle with breast cancer. NBA great Shaquille O'Neal was a surprise participant in the André the Giant Memorial Battle Royal (eliminating Damien Sandow and having a standoff with fellow seven-footer Big Show before being eliminated himself by about 10 other wrestlers), and the Dallas Cowboys Cheerleaders performed on stage as part of The Rock's grand entrance.

Pre-show[edit]

Three matches were contested on the two-hour long WrestleMania Kickoff pre-show, which was broadcast on the WWE Network, WWE.com, Facebook, Google +, Pintrest and YouTube, with USA Network joining the live coverage for the second hour.

In the first hour, Kalisto defended his WWE United States Championship against Ryback. While Ryback used his size advantage to control most of the match, Kalisto won after executing a Salida del Sol on Ryback after he bumped his head on an exposed turnbuckle.

In the second hour, a 10-Diva tag team match pitted Team Total Divas against Team B.A.D. & Blonde. Brie Bella won the match for Team Total Divas by making Naomi submit to a Yes! lock. Nikki Bella, who is still inactive due to a neck injury, came out to celebrate with her sister and the rest of the team. Next, WWE Hall of Famer Lita unveiled the newly-established WWE Women's Championship, which would replace the WWE Divas Championship. Lita announced that the winner of the triple threat match between Charlotte, Becky Lynch and Sasha Banks later that night would be awarded the title. Finally, The Usos defeated The Dudley Boyz in a tag team match. After the match, The Usos frog splashed the Dudleys through tables.

Main card[edit]

The main card broadcast opened with a seven-man ladder match for Kevin Owens' WWE Intercontinental Championship. Following over 15 minutes of wild action (including Sin Cara being knocked off a ladder by Owens and smashing a prone Stardust through a ladder that was laying across the ring and security barricade) The Miz ascended a ladder in the climax of the match when he was shoved off at the last second by Zack Ryder, who then grabbed the belt to win the match. Ryder celebrated his win by inviting his father (who was sitting in the audience at ringside) into the ring.

Next, Chris Jericho took on AJ Styles. After an evenly contested battle (which saw Styles kick out of Jericho's Codebreaker finisher, and Jericho kick out of Styles' Styles Clash), Jericho countered Styles' Phenomenal Forearm into a mid-air Codebreaker to score the pinfall victory.

Zack Ryder was interviewed backstage by Maria Menounos regarding his Intercontinental Championship victory earlier in the night. Ryder stated that he "lived his dream to become champion".

The New Day faced The League of Nations in a six-man tag team match, in a losing effort

The New Day (Big E, Kofi Kingston, and Xavier Woods) (who made their grand entrance from a huge "Booty-O's" cereal box.) then wrestled The League of Nations (Sheamus, Alberto Del Rio, and Rusev) in a six-man tag team match, with LoN teammate King Barrett at ringside. The League of Nations won the match following outside inference by Barrett when the referee was distracted. Afterwards, Barrett bragged that no three men could beat the League of Nations. WWE Hall of Famers Shawn Michaels, Mick Foley, and Stone Cold Steve Austin, then came down and cleared the ring of Barrett and his cohorts. Austin then delivered a Stunner to Woods, who had tried to persuade the Hall of Famers to dance with him.

The No Holds Barred Street Fight between Brock Lesnar and Dean Ambrose was next. The match saw Lesnar executed a total of thirteen suplexes on Ambrose, while Ambrose used kendo sticks and chairs to try to keep Lesnar down (Ambrose also attempted to use a chainsaw, which didn't start). After Ambrose threw numerous chairs in the ring, he brought in a barbed wire wrapped baseball bat to finish Lesnar off, but Lesnar caught Ambrose with a suplex, and executed an F5 finishing move on the pile of chairs to win the match. Afterwards, Lesnar sprayed the fire extinguisher on Ambrose.

Following the 2016 WWE Hall of Fame class being recognised, Charlotte, Becky Lynch, and Sasha Banks (who had her cousin and newly-minted WWE Hall of Famer Snoop Dogg perform her entrance theme while accompanying her to the ring) wrestled in a Triple Threat match. Originally promoted as being for Charlotte's WWE Divas Championship, the match would instead determine the new WWE Women's Champion, as the Divas title would be retired. The ending of the match saw Charlotte use her Figure-Eight leg lock on Lynch, while Ric Flair (Charlotte's manager and father) prevented Banks from getting in the ring and intervening. Lynch submitted, giving the win and the WWE Women's title to Charlotte.

The Hell in a Cell match between The Undertaker and Shane McMahon was next. If McMahon won, he would get control of Raw, while Undertaker would be barred from wrestling at future WrestleManias. Though Undertaker dominated the bulk of the match, McMahon would keep fighting, finally getting an advantage by reversing Undertaker's Hell's Gate submission move into a Sharpshooter. McMahon then placed a garbage can in front of Undertaker's face and hit his Coast-to-Coast finishing move, which Undertaker kicked out of at two. Undertaker then slammed McMahon through the side of the cell, and the two fought on the floor around ringside. After Undertaker slammed McMahon through a broadcast table to get out of a sleeper hold, McMahon recovered and struck Undertaker in the head with TV monitors and a metal toolbox to keep him down, and placed him across another broadcast table. Shane then climbed all the way up to the top of the 20 foot tall cell, and attempted to hit an elbow drop onto Undertaker, who moved out of the way at the last second, causing McMahon to crash trough the table from 20 feet in the air. Though McMahon would motion for Undertaker to "bring it", he had no strength left to fight, and Undertaker would deliver a Tombstone piledriver and pinned McMahon to win the match. McMahon wound up being carted out of the stadium on a stretcher (while showing a thumbs-up to the crowd) while Undertaker walked on his own two feet and smiled.

Next was the André the Giant Memorial Battle Royal. There were several surprise entrants in the match, including legends Diamond Dallas Page and Tatanka, NXT call-up Baron Corbin making his main roster debut, and NBA legend Shaquille O'Neal as a celebrity entry. O'Neal and Big Show double chokeslammed Kane to start, and the other wrestlers were then thrown aside (though not eliminated) by the two seven-footers. After Fandango and Damien Sandow were eliminated by Show and O'Neal respectively, the two big men fought to the ropes, where the rest of the field ganged up and eliminated them both. In the end, Corbin won the match, last eliminating Kane.

Wyatt Family member Erick Rowan was defeated by The Rock, which claimed the record for the shortest WrestleMania match ever contested (officially announced as six seconds) besting the previous record (11 seconds) set by Kane and Chavo Guererro's match at WrestleMania XXIV

The Rock then made a grand entrance, which included the Dallas Cowboys Cheerleaders and Rock using a flamethrower to set a big sign bearing his name ablaze. Rock claimed that WrestleMania 32 had set the all-time attendance record with 101,763, when he was confronted by The Wyatt Family. After some verbal back-and-forth, Rock (who had his ring gear on underneath his street clothes) challenged them to a match. Wyatt Family member Erick Rowan accepted the challenge and was quickly defeated following a Rock Bottom. WWE recognized the match length as six seconds, setting a new WrestleMania record, though other sources reported seven seconds.up id="cite_ref-65" class="reference">[65]up>up id="cite_ref-66" class="reference">[66]up>up id="cite_ref-observerreport_67-0" class="reference">[67]up> Enraged by this quick victory, the entire Wyatt Family then surrounded the Rock when surprisingly John Cena emerged and helped Rock clear the ring of the Wyatts.up id="cite_ref-WM_32_68-0" class="reference">[68]up> The segment ended with Rock and Cena walking up the ramp and Rock hugging his family before he raised Cena's arm.

Main event[edit]

The main event match saw Triple H defend his WWE World Heavyweight Championship against Roman Reigns. Triple H made a Game of Thrones inspired grand entrance, including his wife/manager Stephanie McMahon declaring herself as the "Queen of Queens". Reigns made his entrance by using his Superman punch on the floor followed by fireworks being set off in and around the exterior of the stadium.

After a fast start by Reigns, Triple H methodically worked over Reigns' left arm. Reigns then rallied by spearing Triple H through the ringside barrier. Triple H recovered and again went after the arm, but Reigns rallied again, hitting another spear on Triple H, but Stephanie pulled out the referee (Chad Patton) after getting to two on the pinfall attempt. Stephanie entered the ring to argue with the referee, when she was inadvertently struck by Reigns' spear, as he was aiming for Triple H who had sidestepped the oncoming Reigns. Triple H executed a Pedigree on Reigns, but secured a two count. Triple H, in disbelief, crawled to Stephanie (who was still suffering the effects of Reigns' spear) who handed him his trademark sledgehammer. The referee warned Triple H that he would get disqualified if he used it, then Reigns hit two Superman Punches. Reigns then ran the ropes, dodged a sledgehammer shot, and responded with a third spear to garner the pinfall victory and win the title for a third time. A wide array of celebratory fireworks was released.

As predicted by multiple critics before the event,[69][70][71] Reigns (despite playing a heroic character) was booed by the WrestleMania crowd during the main event and as the show went off the air with him as champion,[67][72] with the result drawing "bullshit" chants. To placate the angry crowd, WWE had Triple H play to the crowd after broadcasting had concluded.[72]

Reception[edit]

Attendance and revenue[edit]

WWE claimed a record WWE event attendance of 101,763 in AT&T Stadium for WrestleMania 32, but this figure has been disputed by some non-WWE sources (most notably by Dave Meltzer of the Wrestling Observer)

WWE claimed an attendance of 101,763[13] in the AT&T Stadium, which surpassed the previous WWE record, a claimed attendance of 93,173 for WrestleMania III in 1987. Also, WWE reported the event grossing $17.3 million for WWE's highest grossing live event.[13] However, journalist Dave Meltzer reported 93,370 as "the attendance as would be normally announced for an event" with a total 97,769 people in the stadium if one includes stadium personnel, WWE personnel, etc.[21] He endorsed the grossing figure as "either accurate or very close".[73]

Reviews[edit]

WrestleMania 32 received mixed reactions[74][75][76] from critics, who criticized the event for its length and questionable booking decisions. Critics who reviewed both WrestleMania 32 and WWE's NXT TakeOver: Dallas event which occured two days earlier concluded that the NXT event was a greatly superior "professional wrestling show" despite WrestleMania having more "spectacle"; also NXT's match of (the debuting) Shinsuke Nakamura versus Sami Zayn was praised as better than any moment or match from WrestleMania 32.[77][78][79]

Troy L. Smith of cleveland.com wrote that "WrestleMania 32 was a night that defied low expectations, thanks in large part a ton of WWE nostalgia", but added that "some of that early goodwill was squandered by odd booking decisions". Smith felt that the main event was "extremely boring  one of the worst main events ever at WrestleMania", with the ending saw "WWE found itself stuck in a moment it couldn't get out of with more boos than cheers". Ultimately, Smith concluded that "WrestleMania 32 proves Zack Ryder (or anyone) is better than Roman Reigns".[80]

Paul Tamburro of CraveOnline wrote that the show was characterized by "inexplicable booking decisions that actively undermined all storyline progression", adding that "WWE routinely swerved its fans with finishes to matches that were less exciting than what had been expected". He decried the main event as a "dull, slow-paced match" and described Reigns' babyface push as "absurd" and "an embarrassing failed experiment for the company", with "the crowd still being given no reason to feel anything for the guy outside of sheer ambivalence or venomous hatred". Tamburro was also critical of the Shane McMahonUndertaker match, which he described as "thoroughly pointless", and of the booking of Dean Ambrose, writing that "him losing to Lesnar in such a disappointing manner puts a big dent in his value". He had further criticism for the company's over-reliance on past stars, writing that "while the implementation of these big names was a lot of fun, it still happened to the detriment of talent currently on the roster". In contrast, Tamburro praised the Women's Championship match, calling it "triumphant", and described Zack Ryder's unexpected win as "the feel-good moment of the show".[81]

James Caldwell of Pro Wrestling Torch described the event as a "weird show". He rated the main event match 1.5 stars out of 5, questioning why "anyone wanted to sit through this long of a main event" and criticizing "the egos of the McMahon family" for booking the match. Caldwell went on to describe Reigns as "a pet project that would be laughed out of this spot in any other era". Caldwell chose not to rate the Hell in a Cell match, describing it as a "weird deal" with "Shane McMahon, a non-wrestler against Undertaker in one of Takers last WrestleMania matches". The women's title match was rated 3 stars, "strong" other than the finish, which puzzled Caldwell when apparently "Mania isn't a big enough event to pay off Flair's repeated interference with the face(s) conquering".[82]

Nolan Howell of the Canadian Online Explorer described the show as "absurdly bizarre. Strange booking decisions and nothing seemed to be resolved at all, aside from the impromptu Shane vs. Undertaker story which could have been tied up any time". Howell wrote that while "the match quality was fine", the show was "spectacle without a lot of substance". He described the second half of the show as a "train wreck creatively", adding that "WWE just can't do anything right really". Howell described the Women's Championship match as the match of the night, and for the main event commented on the "most predictable" boos for Roman Reigns, with the crowd microphones "suspiciously" going largely silent several times during and after the main event.up id="cite_ref-83" class="reference">[83]up> Dave Scherer of Pro Wrestling Insider commented on responses that WrestleMania 32 was "great", countering that "unless you are putting the framework in place for the future, you will reach a point where most of your assets are gone and you will be in a barren place". This was referencing that "the finishes and character development (or lack thereof)" resulting in stars of the past (Austin, Foley, Michaels, Jericho, Rock, Cena) shining at the expense of current wrestlers (Styles, The New Day, The League of Nations and The Wyatt Family). Scherer also criticized Vince McMahon for ignoring the "clear edict" from fans against Roman Reigns as world champion.up id="cite_ref-84" class="reference">[84]up>

Scott Keith of Sporting News wrote that WrestleMania 32 "ended up being a five-hour slog (seven hours with pregame show) and one of the worst WrestleManias when all was said and done". He wrote that "Roman Reigns showed once again that he's not the guy to be the top star despite Triple H's best efforts to get a 'classic' match out of him, and the ShaneUndertaker match ended up being a 30-minute snoozefest instead of the chaotic mess that everyone wanted". Keith praised the Women's Championship match and Zack Ryder for having his "WrestleMania moment".up id="cite_ref-85" class="reference">[85]up>

Aaron Oster of Rolling Stone wrote that "fans witnessed a roller-coaster ride that brought the WWE Universe from the highest of highs to the lowest of lows. Before looking at the bad, it's worth pointing out that there was also a lot of good". However, Oster felt that while WrestleMania 31 tried to "cater to disgruntled fans", WrestleMania 32 "felt like a six-hour attempt to piss off that same demo", especially the event's ending, which caused fans to feel "betrayed" after "dream endings" at WrestleMania 30 and 31.[72]

Aftermath[edit]

On the first Raw after WrestleMania from American Airlines Center in Dallas, Texas, Mr. McMahon returned to confront the crowd before being interrupted by Shane McMahon, who offered a handshake to his father following his loss the previous night, and went to depart the arena. Vince, saying that "nobody upstages [him]" in reference to Shane receiving adoration from the live audience, granted Shane control of Raw for the evening. Shane granted Apollo Crews his main roster debut, where he defeated Tyler Breeze. The night also saw the main roster debut of Baron Corbin, who faced Dolph Ziggler. The match ended in a draw due to a double countout after Corbin sent Ziggler into the live crowd. He proceeded to deliver the End of Days to Ziggler, following a beatdown.

The New Day defended their WWE Tag Team Championship against Sheamus and King Barrett of The League of Nations. The New Day retained the titles after Kingston gained the pinfall over Barrett. Angered by the loss, the group turned on Barrett, labelling him the group's "weakest link", but were subsequently attacked by The Wyatt Family (Bray Wyatt, Erick Rowan and Braun Strowman).

On April 11 episode of Raw, Shane McMahon announced a tag team tournament to decide the number one contender to The New Day's Tag Team Championships. The Dudley Boyz advanced by defeating The Lucha Dragons while The Usos defeated The Social Outcasts to advance. After the match, The Usos were attacked by the debuting Luke Gallows and Karl Anderson who are recognized as two members of the Bullet Club stable in Japan.

Despite winning his first Intercontinental Championship at WrestleMania, Zack Ryder lost the title to The Miz after an argument between Ryder's father, and Miz's wife, Maryse. The argument served as a distraction to Ryder, allowing Miz to hit him with the Skull-Crushing Finale, and win the title for a fifth time.

A ceremony to welcome the newly introduced WWE Women's Championship followed, with every female wrestler on the main roster present inside the ring. After appearing to congratulate Sasha Banks and Becky Lynch following their match at WrestleMania, Charlotte began to brag about her success the night before. Feeling disrespected, the rest of the women's roster left the ceremony, with Natalya staying behind and confronted Charlotte about the need for humility. After Charlotte disrespected the Hart family, Natalya attacked the champion and locked her in her signature move, the Sharpshooter until Ric Flair pulled his daughter out of the ring.

A rematch of their encounter at WrestleMania between The Usos and The Dudley Boyz commenced in a tables match, with The Dudley Boyz winning the reverse fixture. Soon after, Enzo Amore and Colin Cassady appeared, confronting the The Dudley Boyz, who had left following an altercation.

After winning the WWE World Heavyweight Championship from Triple H the previous night, Roman Reigns issued an open challenge to anyone in the locker room for a shot at the title. After Chris Jericho, AJ Styles, Kevin Owens and Sami Zayn all voiced their interest at a potential title shot, Shane scheduled a fatal four-way match between Jericho, Styles, Owens and Zayn to determine the #1 contender for Reigns' title. During a backstage interview, Zayn was attacked by Owens, who hit Zayn with a powerbomb through a table, rendering him unable to compete in the match. Zayn was then replaced with a returning Cesaro in the four-way match. Styles won the match, pinning Jericho after a Styles Clash.[86]

After helping Total Divas win their match at WrestleMania, Brie Bella announced that she was taking an extended break from wrestling on April 6, 2016, so that she could focus on starting a family with her retired husband Daniel Bryan.[87]
